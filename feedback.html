"Asymptotic notations (Big O, Omega, Theta) are mathematical tools used to characterize the running time of an algorithm in terms of the input size $n$. They focus on the growth rate by suppressing constant factors and lower-order terms. For instance, $T(n) = 3n^2 + 5n$ is expressed as $O(n^2)$ to indicate its quadratic complexity."
